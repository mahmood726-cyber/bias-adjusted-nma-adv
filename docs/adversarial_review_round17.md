# Hardcore Methodological Review (Round 17): Causal Inference on Reconstructed Pseudo-IPD

This document registers the transcript of the seventeenth-round multiperson adversarial review, analyzing the information boundary of Guyot-reconstructed Individual Patient Data (IPD) and its impact on the validity of Targeted Minimum Loss-Based Estimation (TMLE) and G-computation.

---

## 1. The Information Boundary of Guyot-Reconstructed IPD

**Dr. Cynthia Registry:**
> "We must establish a strict boundary regarding what the Guyot algorithm actually reconstructs. 
> 
> The Guyot algorithm takes digitized Kaplan-Meier survival curves and numbers-at-risk tables and outputs patient-level survival records. However, these records contain **only three columns**:
> 1.  Survival Time ($t_i$)
> 2.  Event Indicator ($d_i$, where $1$ is event and $0$ is censored)
> 3.  Treatment Arm ($A_i$)
> 
> **It does not and cannot reconstruct baseline patient-level covariates ($W_i$, such as age, renal function, or blood pressure)** because this information is not present in KM curves. Therefore, we do not have true individual patient-level covariate matrices."

---

## 2. How TMLE and G-Computation Operate on Synthetic Covariates

**Dr. Benjamin MCMC:**
> "Since we lack true covariate matrices, our causal models (TMLE and G-computation) cannot run on raw clinical patient files. Instead, they rely on our **Survival VAE cohort simulator**.
> 
> The VAE takes the aggregate baseline covariate tables reported in published trials (means and standard deviations) and generates a **synthetic patient cohort** ($W_{synthetic}$) matching those distributions. 
> 
> When we run TMLE:
> *   The propensity score model $g(W)$ is fit on these synthetic covariates.
> *   The outcome model $Q(A, W)$ predicts event probabilities over the synthetic cohort.
> 
> *The Methodological Caveat:* The resulting TMLE risk difference is **conditional on the synthetic covariate distributions generated by the VAE**, rather than a true patient-level causal transport. It is a mathematical approximation of the target trial, not a direct causal extraction."

---

## 3. Data Grounding: Real-World vs. Reconstructed/Synthetic Boundary

To comply with the project integrity rules (AGENTS.md), we declare exactly which data elements represent true source-backed registry observations versus mathematical approximations:

| Data Element | Source / Registry | Classification | Mathematical Role / Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Efficacy Event Rates / Sample Sizes** | ClinicalTrials.gov results | **Source-Backed Real Data** | Anchors the exact sample sizes ($N$) and event counts. |
| **Digitized KM Coordinates** | Published PDF figures | **Source-Backed Real Data** | Input to the IPD reconstructor. |
| **Patient Survival Times ($t_i, d_i$)** | Guyot Reconstruction | **Reconstructed Approximation** | Back-calculated via Faithful Guyot; verified via IAE Wasserstein audit. |
| **Patient Baseline Covariates ($W_i$)** | Survival VAE Simulator | **Synthetic Model Data** | Generated to match baseline table means and standard deviations; feeds the TMLE propensity model. |

---

## Final Recommendation: Honest Reporting of Synthetic Boundaries

**Panel Consensus:**
> "While TMLE and G-computation are mathematically correct, they must be presented as **exploratory causal approximations** due to the absence of true patient-level covariates. The VAE-based covariate simulation allows the models to run, but the resulting risk differences must always be reported alongside the synthetic data disclosure table."
